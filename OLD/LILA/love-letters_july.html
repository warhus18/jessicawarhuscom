LILA Opens in Manayunk!

Last month, a longtime dream became a reality. We took the leap and opened our first brick and mortar boutique in Manayunk, at 4386 Main Street. We felt Manayunk was the right fit for us - the community is so rich in character and people, so vibrant and artistic - it charmed the pants right off us! Seeing the space for the first time, it had an aura of possibility. Call it positive vibes, a gut feeling, or too much chai tea and yoga - but we were sold. And the rest is, as they say, history.

We're incredibly grateful for the recent news coverage we've received for LILA! Get the scoop on our website.

New Designer Madame Shou Shou

Madame Shou Shou is the quintessential fairytale brand. The fashion house (founded by Athens-born Elena Kordali) plays with floral patterns, rich velvet and delicate lace to tell their story. Their newest collection will undoubtedly romance you. Think ultra-feminine pieces with long, flowing lines and embellishments. We love Madame Shou Shou because everything is made in-house; even the fabric patterns are made exclusively for her garments. This season Kordali's swimsuits, which include floral one-pieces with a plunging backline and bikinis made with water-tolerable velvet, are sure to turn a few heads at the beach. Bask in the sun or head out to sea styling the boudoir-inspired Shark, the darling Dolphin or playful Salmon suit. Madame Shou Shou doesn't stop at beachwear - the garments are equally spellbinding! From bouncy shorts, to rompers and sweet mini dresses, every piece is lightweight - perfect for summertime and for traveling.

PHILLY LOVE -
HAPPY INDEPENDENCE DAY!

Mmmm July - the month of red, white and blue, sparklers, cookouts and leisurely afternoons on the beach. Residing in Philadelphia this is a particularly exciting month given the city's rich history. LILA's hometown played an instrumental role in the American Revolution - HQ to the Founding Fathers of the Union. The Declaration of Independence was signed here in 1776 and the Constitution in 1787.
